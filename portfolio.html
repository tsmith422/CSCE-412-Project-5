Rev Bot

I was tasked by the Texas Engineering Academies Student Organization to create a Discord bot for automating role assignments after becoming an officer. Despite my Python knowledge, I had never created a Discord bot or worked with an API, making this a great learning opportunity.

The organization requires a one-time payment for students to access important Discord channels, and members who pay gain the "Verified" role. The bot uses the Google Sheets API to automate role assignments by verifying members' information from a form saved on a spreadsheet.

This method allows real-time updates and immediate verification, along with 24/7 uptime due to the process running on a rasbian comparable microcomputer. While the bot primarily handles new member authentication, I plan to expand its functionality for other tasks and ensure the code is understandable for future officers.

Wordle

In my sophomore year of college, I created my first visual interactive program using JavaFX and SceneBuilder, which made the design process enjoyable and straightforward. With my Java experience, I focused on adding features and creating a visually appealing Wordle game.

The project involved selecting random words through Java File IO, using a complex data structure for letter storage, and ensuring the application was user-friendly. Additionally, I implemented player statistics tracking, including wins/losses, number of games played, and streaks, all accessible at any time.

2023 Datathon

I participated in the 2023 TAMU Datathon, my first-ever datathon, where our team trained an AI model to predict patient survival based on their condition. We worked with a flawed dataset in CSV format, cleaned and adjusted the data, and used recursive feature elimination to identify key survival factors.

This enabled us to train the Keras Sequential Model for predictions, which were then scored for accuracy. Despite our code not being the most accurate, our detailed writeup helped our team, consisting of Arul Dhar, Bao Nguyen, and myself, to be selected as one of the winners of the Datathon.
