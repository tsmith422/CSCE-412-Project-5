TEASO Officer/Ambassador

During my time in the Texas Engineering Academies Student Organization (TEASO), I've had the opportunity manage communications technologies and collaborated with the treasurer to streamline the membership processes.

Additionally, I have served as an ambassador, providing guidance and support to incoming students, sharing my experiences, and promoting the benefits of the Texas A&M Engineering Academies.

Wished to be an Undergratuate Teaching Assistant

Ones I Looked Up to

During the spring semester of my sophomore year in college, I took one of the more challenging programming courses I had ever encountered. The course covered very difficult topics, but the undergraduate teaching assistants (TAs) were extremely helpful. They made the process of learning and understanding the material much easier and provided ample resources to ensure we grasped the concepts.

My Appreciation

I really appreciated the way the TAs taught us. They were patient, knowledgeable, and always willing to help. Their dedication and support made a significant difference in my learning experience. When given the opportunity, I applied for the same position, but for a different class, hoping to make a similar impact on other students.

Aspiration to Help Others

Unfortunately, it did not work out, but I still had high hopes of helping someone else learn something challenging, just as my TAs had helped me. I remain inspired by their teaching methods and the positive influence they had on my education.
